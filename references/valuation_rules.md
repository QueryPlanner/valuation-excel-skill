# Valuation review rules

## Story before numbers

Write the operating story before approving assumptions. State how the company
grows, how margins evolve, what reinvestment is required, how competitive
advantages fade or persist, and which risks can impair value.

Do not change assumptions merely to move estimated value toward market price.

## Growth

Compare next-year growth with:

- recent organic growth,
- management guidance and backlog,
- industry growth,
- capacity and customer constraints,
- the implied year-10 revenue and market share.

Fade years 2–5 growth toward a sustainable rate unless expansion or durable
share gains provide documented support.

## Profitability

Compare next-year and target operating margins with:

- current and historical margins,
- segment mix,
- normalized one-time costs,
- scale economics,
- industry distribution and mature peers.

Explain any target outside the industry interquartile range.

## Reinvestment

Estimate sales-to-capital from historical incremental revenue and invested
capital. Adjust for acquisitions and major asset disposals. Confirm that
reinvestment can finance the stated growth.

## Risk and cost of capital

Review:

- valuation currency and risk-free rate,
- geographic revenue-weighted ERP,
- business mix and unlevered beta,
- debt maturity and default spread,
- market-value capital weights,
- tax treatment,
- consistency between stable growth and stable risk.

## Terminal assumptions

Terminal growth must be economically sustainable for the valuation currency.
The terminal cost of capital and return on capital must reflect mature-company
risk and competitive fade unless documented evidence supports an exception.

## Required challenge

Before approval, identify:

1. The assumption with the largest effect on value.
2. The strongest evidence against the base case.
3. The market-implied assumption that explains the price/value gap.
4. The disclosure or data limitation most likely to change the result.
